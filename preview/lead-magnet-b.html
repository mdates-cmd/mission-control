--- # 5 AI Prompts That Replace Your VA This Week ### By The Deal Flow AI Team ## How to use this guide Copy the prompt you need, replace the bracketed inputs with your details, and run it inside ChatGPT or your preferred AI writing assistant. For best results, be specific with property details, market context, and desired tone. --- ## Prompt 1: Listing Description Generator **Use this for:** MLS remarks, listing pages, email blasts, and flyer copy. **Prompt:** ```text You are an expert real estate copywriter. Write an MLS-ready listing description for the following property: - Address: [INSERT ADDRESS] - Beds/Baths: [INSERT BEDS/BATHS] - Square Footage: [INSERT SQ FT] - Property Type: [INSERT PROPERTY TYPE] - Key Features: [INSERT FEATURES] - Neighborhood or Area Highlights: [INSERT AREA DETAILS] - Ideal Buyer: [INSERT TARGET BUYER] - Tone: [professional / luxury / family-friendly / investor-focused] Requirements: 1. Keep the description between 120 and 180 words. 2. Lead with the strongest selling angle. 3. Highlight the most valuable features naturally. 4. Avoid fair housing issues or prohibited language. 5. End with a compelling call to action. Output: - Headline option - MLS description - 3 alternate feature bullets for marketing use ``` **Why it works:** It gives the AI the structure, tone, and compliance guardrails it needs to produce usable copy fast. --- ## Prompt 2: Seller Outreach Email Writer **Use this for:** Cold outreach, warm follow-up, probate leads, absentee owners, or distressed property leads. **Prompt:** ```text You are writing a personalized real estate seller outreach email. Use the following lead information: - Seller Name: [INSERT NAME] - Property Address: [INSERT ADDRESS] - Lead Type: [absentee owner / inherited property / tired landlord / FSBO / pre-foreclosure / other] - Motivation Clues: [INSERT DETAILS] - Timeframe: [INSERT TIMEFRAME] - My Offer or Value Proposition: [INSERT VALUE PROP] - Tone: [professional / warm / direct] Write an outreach email that: 1. Feels personal, not mass produced. 2. Acknowledges the seller's likely situation without sounding invasive. 3. Explains how I can help in a clear and low-pressure way. 4. Includes a strong but simple call to action. 5. Stays under 180 words. Output: - Subject line options (3) - Main email draft - Short follow-up variation if they do not reply ``` **Why it works:** It blends personalization with speed, which is exactly where most seller outreach breaks down. --- ## Prompt 3: Offer Letter Generator **Use this for:** Investor offers, wholesale offers, buyer representation communication, or any professional summary of terms. **Prompt:** ```text You are writing a professional real estate offer letter. Property and offer details: - Recipient Name: [INSERT NAME] - Property Address: [INSERT ADDRESS] - Purchase Price: [INSERT PRICE] - Earnest Money: [INSERT EMD] - Financing Type: [cash / conventional / hard money / other] - Inspection Period: [INSERT TERMS] - Closing Timeline: [INSERT TIMELINE] - Special Terms or Conditions: [INSERT TERMS] - Buyer Positioning Notes: [why this offer is strong] - Tone: [professional / confident / relationship-focused] Write a concise and polished offer letter that: 1. Clearly summarizes the key terms. 2. Sounds credible and professional. 3. Reduces confusion or friction. 4. Ends with a courteous call to discuss or accept. Output: - Offer letter - Short version for email body - 3 subject line options ``` **Why it works:** It helps you communicate clearly and professionally without rewriting the same structure every time. --- ## Prompt 4: Lead Follow-Up Sequence Writer **Use this for:** New inquiries, warm leads, old leads, website leads, or unresponsive prospects. **Prompt:** ```text You are building a 3-touch follow-up sequence for a real estate lead. Lead details: - Lead Name: [INSERT NAME] - Lead Source: [website / Zillow / referral / Facebook / open house / other] - Last Contact Date: [INSERT DATE] - Lead Interest: [buying / selling / investing / renting / financing] - Known Context: [INSERT NOTES] - Desired Tone: [helpful / direct / consultative] Create a 3-message follow-up sequence: 1. Message 1 should re-open the conversation. 2. Message 2 should add value or clarity. 3. Message 3 should create urgency or offer an easy next step. Requirements: - Keep each message under 90 words. - Make the language natural and human. - Do not sound desperate or generic. - Include suggested send timing for each message. Output: - Touch 1 - Touch 2 - Touch 3 - Optional subject lines or text variations ``` **Why it works:** Most leads die from inconsistent follow-up, not lack of interest. This prompt fixes that fast. --- ## Prompt 5: Market Update Generator **Use this for:** Client emails, monthly newsletters, investor updates, listing presentations, and social content drafts. **Prompt:** ```text You are a real estate market analyst writing a client-ready local market update. Use these local stats: - Market Area: [INSERT AREA] - Median Sale Price: [INSERT PRICE] - Average Days on Market: [INSERT DOM] - Inventory Level: [INSERT INVENTORY] - Month-over-Month Change: [INSERT CHANGE] - Year-over-Year Change: [INSERT CHANGE] - Buyer or Seller Trend Notes: [INSERT NOTES] - Audience: [buyers / sellers / investors / general audience] - Tone: [professional / educational / concise] Write a market update that: 1. Explains the data in plain English. 2. Highlights what changed and why it matters. 3. Gives the audience 2 or 3 practical takeaways. 4. Ends with a clear invitation to connect. Output: - Email version - Short newsletter summary - 3 social post hooks based on the update ``` **Why it works:** It turns raw numbers into useful communication your audience can actually act on. --- ## Final note These prompts are designed to save time immediately, but the bigger win is consistency. Once your messaging, outreach, and content workflows are systemized, you stop relying on memory, mood, or spare time to keep deals moving. If you want the next layer beyond prompts, build the workflow behind them.
